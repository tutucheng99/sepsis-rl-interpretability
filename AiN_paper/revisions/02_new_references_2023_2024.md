### For Section 4.5 (Future Directions) - Expanded paragraph:
> "Additionally, Transformer-based reinforcement learning methods represent an emerging paradigm for clinical decision support. The Medical Decision Transformer (MeDT)^37^ has been specifically developed for sepsis treatment recommendation, utilizing goal-conditioned reinforcement learning to predict drug dosages. The Continuous-Time Decision Transformer^38^ extends this approach to handle irregular time intervals common in clinical data, suggesting both visit timings and treatment plans. These methods leverage the powerful attention mechanisms that have proven successful in natural language processing^40^. Future work should investigate whether Transformer-based RL can achieve both strong performance and interpretability in clinical decision support^41^, potentially combining sequence modeling capabilities with the conservative value estimation principles that make CQL interpretable.
>
> Furthermore, recent advances in safe offline RL^36^ address the critical challenge of out-of-distribution handling in healthcare applications, proposing frameworks like Offline Guarded Safe Reinforcement Learning (OGSRL) that demonstrated a 78% reduction in mortality estimates compared to clinician decisions. Integrating such safety mechanisms with our interpretability framework represents a promising direction for clinically deployable AI systems^39,42^."
