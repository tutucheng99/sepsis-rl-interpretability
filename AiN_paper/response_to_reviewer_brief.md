# Response to Reviewer

**Reviewer's Comment:** *"The recent rise of generative artificial intelligence requests its due attention. Hence, I would like to ask the authors to conduct additional analysis based on generative artificial intelligence..."*

**Response:**

We thank the reviewer for this thoughtful suggestion regarding generative artificial intelligence. Our study focuses specifically on reinforcement learning (RL) for sequential clinical decision-making in sepsis treatment, comparing traditional offline RL methods (Behavior Cloning, Conservative Q-Learning) with online RL methods (DQN variants, SAC), with the core contribution being a systematic interpretability benchmark using Linearly Estimated Gradients (LEG) analysis. While generative AI methods such as GANs and Transformers have achieved remarkable success in image generation and natural language processing, they address fundamentally different problems—data generation or classification rather than sequential treatment decisions over time. We acknowledge that Transformer architectures have been adapted for reinforcement learning; notably, Decision Transformer (Chen et al., 2021) reformulates RL as a sequence modeling problem, and we have cited this work in our manuscript (Reference 34) and discussed it as a promising future direction. To address the reviewer's suggestion, we have expanded our Future Directions section to more explicitly acknowledge the potential of Transformer-based RL methods, noting that future work should investigate whether these approaches can achieve both strong performance and interpretability in clinical decision support. We respectfully submit that incorporating full GAN analyses would extend beyond the scope of our current study, as GANs optimize for realistic sample generation rather than providing the feature-level explanations of treatment recommendations that are essential for regulatory approval and clinical trust in healthcare AI.
